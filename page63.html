Uses of the Sutra

1. Multiplications of any number by any number:

The sutra simplifies the multiplications having 2 × 2, 3 × 3, and 4 × 4, and so on.

2. Division of Large Numbers:

The sutra simplifies the division of large numbers, including decimals and binary numbers, by working backward from the product of the digits using vertical and crosswise operations.

3. Solving Simultaneous Linear Equations:

Consistency of linear equations can be checked using the determinant and adjoint properties derived using the sutra.

It is instrumental in solving equations in quantum chemistry, such as predicting molecular shapes.

4. Calculus Operations:

Useful for calculating the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd order derivatives of the product of two or more functions.

Example: In thermodynamics, calculating changes in Gibbs free energy or entropy with temperature.

5. Integration of Product Functions:

Simplifies integration tasks, which is critical in reaction rate calculations and understanding equilibrium constants.

1. Stepwise Explanation of Multiplication Using the Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyām Sutra

Example: Multiply 243 × 154.

Align the numbers vertically and apply the sutra step by step:

Table 3.1 Step-by-step multiplication by using Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyām Sutra

Steps Calculation Result
1 Multiply the first digits vertically: 2 × 1 2
2 Multiply crosswise and sum: (2 × 5) + (4 × 1) 14
3 Multiply all digits crosswise: (2 × 4) + (4 × 5) + (3 × 1) 31
4 Multiply the last two digits crosswise and vertically: (4 × 4) + (3 × 5) 31
5 Multiply the last digits vertically: 3 × 4 12

Combine the results (considering the carryovers): 37422.

Thus, 243 × 154 = 37422.
