Sutra 1. Ekādhikena Pürvena (एकािधके न पूवőण)

Meaning: By one more than the one before.

Use: This sutra is used to get the next number to a given number.

The first sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Ekādhikena Pūrvena, is a powerful mental tool that translates to "by one more than the previous one." This concise yet profound principle is primarily used for rapid calculations, particularly when finding the square of numbers ending in 5. In mathematics, this sutra enables learners to square the numbers like 25, 35, or 95 instantly, without needing paper or calculators. The simplicity of multiplying the preceding digit by its successor and appending "25" at the end transforms the way students approach squaring problems.

Beyond pure mathematics, Ekādhikena Pūrvena finds practical value in science as well. In chemistry, where accurate calculations of quantities such as theoretical and percentage yields are critical, this sutra can simplify steps when squaring values is required, like estimating squared values in molar mass comparisons, energy levels, or yield-based computations. This method eliminates complexity, allowing students to focus on interpreting the results rather than being slowed down by computation. Thus, this sutra not only sharpens arithmetic skills but also supports faster, more confident problem-solving in scientific contexts for enhancing numerical fluency where precision and speed are essential.

Example: To get the next number to 16, Add 1 to 16, thus next number = 16 + 1 = 17.

This sutra is particularly useful in finding the square of a number that ends with 5.

Let's take 25 2 as an example:

Step 1: Observe the first digit(s) of the number before the 5. For 24, the first digit is 2.

Step 2: Add 1 to this digit 2. This gives 3.

Step 3: Multiply the original digit (2) by the number you obtained in Step 2 (3).

This gives 2 × 3 = 6

Step 4: Write "25" at the end of this product. Hence, 25 2 = 625
