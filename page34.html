Sutra 10. Yavadunam ( यावदूनम् )

Meaning: By the deficiency.

Use: This sutra is used in the following two cases.

The tenth sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Yāvadūnam, provides a powerful approach to simplify calculations by using the concept of deficiency from a base number. This sutra is typically applied in two major contexts: calculating complements to the nearest base and performing efficient cubing of numbers through a digit-based method. The sutra structures the cube as a series of manageable sub-calculations based on the digits of the number, which are then combined using straightforward arithmetic. Additionally, its application to finding complements is useful in subtraction and error-checking in numeric systems.

Beyond arithmetic, this sutra demonstrates practical utility in scientific computations. In chemistry and physics, volume-related problems often require cube calculations such as determining the volume of atoms, molecules, or crystalline structures.
