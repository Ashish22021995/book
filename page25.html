Example 3: Chemical Kinetics (Additional Simultaneous Equation Example)

Problem:The rate laws for two reactions are given as:

r1 = k1 [A]2 + k2 [B] = 500

r2 = 2k1 [A]2+ 2k2 [B] = 1000

Find the value of [B].

Solution:

1. Simplify the Equations:

First equation: k1[A]2 + k2[B] = 500,

Second equation: 2k1[A]2 + 2k2[B] = 1000

2. Compare the Ratios of Coefficients:

Coefficients of [A]2 = 1:2,

Constants: 500:1000 = 1:2.

3. Apply the Sutra: Since the ratios are equal, by Ānyarūpaye Śūnyamanyat, [B] = 0.

Result: The solution is [B] = 0, meaning that the reaction rate depends only on [A].

Ānyarūpaye Śūnyamanyat simplifies systems of equations by identifying proportional relationships. If one set of ratios matches, the other variable must be zero. This is especially helpful in scientific problems like chemical equilibrium, kinetics, and molar relationships, allowing for quicker solutions and clearer insight into variable dependencies.
