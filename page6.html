In scientific contexts, especially in chemistry, this sutra holds practical relevance. It can be applied in calculations where determining the remaining or unreacted quantity of a substance is necessary. During experimental processes or analytical tasks, students often need to subtract consumed amounts from a total, and using this sutra makes that process significantly quicker. This efficiency is not just a matter of speed but also contributes to greater focus on conceptual understanding and interpretation of scientific data. Ultimately, Nikhilam Navataścaramam Daśataḥ bridges numerical simplicity with practical application, equipping learners with a mental strategy that supports fast, accurate, and confident problem-solving in both academic and real-world scientific settings.

Steps to Apply:

Step 1: Identify the functional base (nearest power of 10 ) of the given number.

Step 2: Subtract each digit of the number from 9, except the last digit, which is subtracted from 10.

Step 3: Write down the complement obtained.

Example 1: Find 100 − 47

1. Functional base: 100.

2. Subtract:

  • 9 − 4 = 5 (first digit)
  • 10 − 7 = 3 (last digit)

Complement: 53.

Result: 100 – 47 = 53.

Example 2: Find 1000 − 358

1. Functional base: 1000.

2. Subtract:

  • 9 − 3 = 6 (first digit)
  • 9 − 5 = 4 (last digit)
  • 10 − 8 = 2 (last digit)

Complement: 642.

Result: 1000 – 358 = 642.
