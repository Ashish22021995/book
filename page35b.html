Beyond arithmetic, this sutra demonstrates practical utility in scientific computations. In chemistry and physics, volume-related problems often require cube calculations such as determining the volume of atoms, molecules, or crystalline structures. Yāvadūnam simplifies these computations, making it an effective tool when calculating volumes based on atomic radii or edge lengths of unit cells. For instance, the sutra helps derive precise values when computing r3 in spherical volume formulas or in stoichiometry where side lengths of molecular units are cubed to estimate total volume.

Case 1: Finding the Complement to the Nearest Functional Base

Example: Find the complement of 118 to the nearest base (100).

Solution:

1. Identify the functional base: The nearest base to 118 is 100.

2. Calculate the complement: Complement = Base − Number = 100 − 118 = −18

3. In absolute terms: Complement = 18

Answer: The complement of 118 to the base 100 is ±18
