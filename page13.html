Example: Solve 8x+3=5x+6

1. Rearrange: 8x − 5x = 6 − 3 (transpose terms and reverse signs).

2. Simplify: 3x = 3.

3. Solve: x = 3/3 = 1.

3. Polynomial Division

Steps to Apply:

1. Write the polynomial division in synthetic form.

2. Keep the leading coefficient as it is, and reverse the signs of subsequent coefficients based on
the divisor.

3. Solve step-by-step until all terms are divided.

Example: Divide x3−7x2 + 41x−35 by x−1

1. Divisor: x−1 → Transform by keeping the sign of 1 as it is.

2. Write coefficients of the polynomial:

[1, −7, 41, −35].

3. Perform synthetic division:

The first coefficient (1) remains the same. [1, −7, 41, −35].

Multiply 1×1 and add to −7: −7+1= −6.

Multiply −6×1 and add to 41: 41−6=35.

Multiply 35×1 and add to −35: −35+35=0.
