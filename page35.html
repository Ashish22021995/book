Case 1: Finding the Complement to the Nearest Functional Base

Example: Find the complement of 118 to the nearest base (100).

Solution:

1. Identify the functional base: The nearest base to 118 is 100.

2. Calculate the complement: Complement = Base − Number = 100 − 118 = −18

3. In absolute terms: Complement = 18

Answer: The complement of 118 to the base 100 is ±18

Case 2: Finding the Cube of a Two-Digit Number

1. Split the number into digits:

Let the number xy have:

x = tens digit, y = units digit.

2. Compute the six terms:

Calculate the following:

x 3: Cube of the tens digit.

x 2y: Square of the tens digit multiplied by the units digit.

xy 2: Tens digit multiplied by the square of the unit digit.

y 3: Cube of the units’ digit.

2x 2: Twice the product of x2 and y.

2xy 2: Twice the product of xxx and y2.

3. Arrange the terms:

Write them in the order:

x3
(x2y + 2x2y)
(xy2 + 2xy2)
y3

4. Handle carryovers:

Add the terms column by column, carrying over any extra digits to the next column.

5. Write the final result:

The digits from the sums and carryovers give the cube of the number.

Example: Find the cube of 12

1. Split 12 into digits x = 1 and y = 2.

2. Compute each term:

x 3 = 1 3 = 1

x 2y = 1 2 × 2 = 2

xy 2 = 1 × 2 2 = 4
