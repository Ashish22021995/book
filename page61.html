In Chemistry, calculating molar masses, balancing equations, or applying mole concepts involves ratios and proportions in these areas where Anurupyena (proportionality) and Shishyate-Sheshasangnah (constant remainder) can be applied with ease. Similarly, in Physics, formulas involving velocity, displacement, and acceleration often require squaring or multiplying values quickly, which sub-sutras can handle effortlessly. These sutras also build a strong foundation for manipulating equations, solving quadratic or simultaneous systems, and simplifying expressions all of which are common in scientific analysis and derivation.

Moreover, sub-sutras encourage mental flexibility and pattern recognition skills essential for scientific inquiry. Sutras like Vilokanam (by mere observation) train students to recognize numerical relationships or approximate values at a glance, which is particularly useful in estimation and scientific measurements. Instead of relying solely on calculators, students can mentally estimate values, increasing speed during exams and fostering deeper numerical intuition. Overall, integrating the sub-sutras of Vedic Mathematics into science education not only enhances computational efficiency but also nurtures logical thinking, precision, and confidence qualities that are indispensable in any scientific pursuit.
