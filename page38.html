Solution:

1. Determine the Volume of a Single Atom: Using the cube of 2.3 (side length of the cubic atom) to find the volume:

V atom = (side length) 3 = (2.3Å) 3

Using the Yavadunam Sutra, we calculated:

(2.3Å) 3 = 12.167 Å 3 = 12.167 × 10 -24 cm 3

2. Total Volume of All Atoms: Multiply the volume of one atom by the total number of

atoms in the molecule:

V total = V atom × N

Where N is the number of atoms.

Substituting the value:

V total = 12.167 × 10 -24 cm 3 × 6.022 × 10 23

This gives the total volume occupied by all atoms.

V total = 7.32 cm 3

The Yāvadūnam Sutra demonstrates the elegance of mental arithmetic through its structured approach to complements and cubing. In scientific domains like chemistry and physics, this sutra proves especially useful for simplifying volume-based computations. From calculating the cube of atomic radii in crystalline structures to evaluating molar volumes and stoichiometric relationships, Yāvadūnam brings speed, clarity, and precision to otherwise complex calculations. It empowers learners to visualize numerical transformations and apply them directly in real-world scientific analysis.

Sutra 11. Vyasti Samastih ( व्यष्टि समष्टिः )

Meaning: Specific and general or Whole as one and One as whole

Use: The sutra enables solving special bi-quadratic equations by removing odd power

terms (e.g., cubic or linear terms) and simplifying the equation into a manageable form, often by averaging terms or dividing the middle term into two quadratic equations.
