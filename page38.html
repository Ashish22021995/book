The eleventh sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Vyasti Samastih, introduces a strategic approach to solving bi-quadratic equations by transforming them into symmetric forms. This sutra is especially valuable in simplifying expressions where two binomial terms differ by a constant and their powers generate complex polynomial equations. The method involves taking the average of binomial expressions, thereby converting the problem into a more manageable form. When applied to fourth powers (bi-quadratics), this sutra effectively cancels out the odd-powered terms through algebraic symmetry. The resulting equation typically contains only even powers, which can then be further simplified into a quadratic form using substitution. Solving this quadratic yields intermediate values that are then traced back to determine the original unknowns.

Vyasti Samastih has practical value beyond theoretical algebra. In scientific applications, the energy expressions involving symmetrical orbitals or related particle interactions often involve fourth power terms. This sutra simplifies such expressions when energy levels are centered on a mean value, offering a systematic pathway to compute possible energy states efficiently. Example: Solve

(x + 7) 4 + (x + 5) 4 = 706

Step 1: Take the average of the two binomials

The two binomials are x + 7 and x + 5.

Find their average:

x + 6 (Let this be a)

Thus, x + 7 + a + 1 and x + 5 = a - 1

Step 2: Replace the binomials in the equation

(x + 7) 4 + (x + 5) 4 = (a + 1) 4 + (a - 1) 4 = 706

Step 3: Expand and cancel odd powers

Expand both terms:

(a + 1) 4 = a 4 + 4a 3 + 6a 2 + 4a + 1

(a - 1) 4 = a 4 - 4a 3 + 6a 2 - 4a + 1

Add these:

(a + 1) 4 + (a - 1) 4 = 2a 4 + 12a 2 + 2 = 706 (Odd powers cancle out)
