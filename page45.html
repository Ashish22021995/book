Sutra 13. Sopantyadvayamantyam ( सोपान्त्यद्वयमन्तयम् )

Meaning: The ultimate and twice the penultimate.

Use: This sutra focuses on operations involving the ultimate digit (last digit) and penultimate digit (second-to-last digit). It is primarily used for:

1. Checking divisibility by 4.

Add the last digit and twice the second-last digit of the number.

If the sum is divisible by 4, the entire number is divisible by 4.

2. Simplifying multiplication by 12 using a systematic approach.

Add a zero before and after the number.

Work step-by-step, adding the last digit and twice the second-last digit, accounting for carryovers.

The thirteenth sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Sopāntyadvayamantyam, reveals a method grounded in positional digit manipulation specifically focusing on the last digit (ultimate) and the second-to-last digit (penultimate) of numbers. Though subtle in appearance, this technique provides insightful shortcuts for arithmetic processes like divisibility checks and rapid multiplication, as well as algebraic simplifications. This approach breaks down what might seem like a complex multiplication into manageable steps by operating on digit pairs. It also deals with rational expressions involving products of binomials arranged in an arithmetic progression.

In scientific domains, the sutra extends its value by aiding in problems involving structured sequences. For example, ionization energy trends in chemistry. When successive ionization energies form an arithmetic progression, Sopāntyadvayamantyam helps to deduce unknown values from symmetric expressions that would otherwise require multi-step algebraic work. By merging digit-wise logic with pattern recognition, this sutra empowers learners to handle arithmetic and algebraic challenges with precision.
