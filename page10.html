Application of Urdhva-tiryagbhyam in Chemistry: Number of Molecules in a Compound

Problem: A laboratory sample contains 34g of water (H₂O). The molar mass of water is 18g/mol. Calculate the total number of water molecules in the sample using 34×56, where 56 represents Avogadro's constant in simplified form (6.022×1023) divided by 18.

Solution:

1. Given Values:

Mass of water: 34 g

Simplified value for (Avogadro’s constant)/(Molar Mass) = 56

2. Apply the Sutra to Calculate 34×56:

Write the numbers: 34 and 56.

Step-by-Step Multiplication Using the Sutra:

Step 1: Multiply the unit digits: 4×6=24. Write 4 in the unit's place. Carry over 2.

Step 2: Multiply crosswise: (3×6)+(4×5)=18+20=38.
Add the carry-over 2: 38+2=40. Write 0 in the tens place and carry over 4.

Step 3: Multiply the tens digits: 3×5=15. Add the carry-over 4: 15+4=19.

Combine the results: 1904.

3. Interpretation: The total number of water molecules in the sample is approximately: 1.904×1023 molecules.

Complement (Unreacted mass) = 3258 mg.
