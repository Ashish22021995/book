4.1. Stepwise Explanation of Integration Using the Ūrdhva-Tiryagbhyām Sutra

Using the standard formulas for derivatives and integration, the integration of the product of two functions can be efficiently determined.

For example,

Consider,

I = ∫(x + 3)2 (ex) dx

Here, let u = (x + 3)2, perform successive differentiation of u until it reduces to zero.

Simultaneously, compute successive integrations of the other function v = ex(e.g., double, triple, etc.). Arrange the results in a tabular format and apply the Crosswise Sutra, alternating the signs for each term to arrive at the final solution.
