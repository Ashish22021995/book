This sutra enables efficient solutions to systems of equations by focusing on proportional relationships. When applied to appropriate quadratic equations, it helps in directly identifying one of the roots by recognizing the equality of ratios and constants.

In scientific applications especially in chemistry, Ānyarūpaye Śūnyamanyat can assist in simplifying reaction-based relationships, such as determining molar ratios, evaluating reaction kinetics, or solving concentration-based equilibrium problems. By recognizing proportional patterns in equations that describe chemical behavior, this sutra allows students and practitioners to isolate values more intuitively. It becomes particularly useful when analyzing reaction mechanisms or solving for unknowns in rate laws, where balanced proportionality frequently occurs.

Example 1 Simultaneous Equation: Solve the following equations:

210x + 120y = 1050

630x + 360y = 3150

Steps:

Step 1: Compare the ratios of coefficients and constants.

Coefficients of x: 210: 630 = 1: 3.

Constants: 1050: 3150 = 1: 3.

Since the ratios are equal, by Ānurūpye Śūnyamanyat Sūtra, y = 0.

Step 2: Substitute y = 0 into the first equation:
