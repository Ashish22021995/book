Example 3: Find 10000 − 67421

1. Functional base: 10000.

2. Subtract:

  • 9 − 6 = 3 (first digit)
  • 9 − 7 = 2 (last digit)
  • 9 − 4 = 5 (third digit)
  • 10 − 2 = 8 (last digit)

Complement: 3258.

Result: 10000 – 6742 = 3258.

Application of Nikhilam sutra in chemistry: Unreacted Molecule Mass in a Reaction

Problem: A chemical reaction starts with 10000 mg of a reactant. After the reaction, 6742 mg is consumed. Use the Nikhilam Navatas'caramam Dasatah sutra to find the unreacted mass of the molecule.

Solution:

1. Functional base: 10000.

2. Subtract each digit of 6742:

  • 9 − 6 = 3 (first digit)
  • 9 − 7 = 2 (last digit)
  • 9 − 4 = 5 (third digit)
  • 10 − 2 = 8 (last digit)

Complement (Unreacted mass) = 3258 mg.

Nikhilam Navataścaramam Daśataḥ offers a fast and intuitive method for subtraction by finding complements from base values, making it especially effective in scientific tasks like calculating remaining quantities or unreacted substances. It enhances efficiency, accuracy, and mental clarity in both academic and non-academic settings.
