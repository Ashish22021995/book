In the present chapter, the researcher has reviewed the related literature and research conducted in the past. A literature review is a complete summary of previous research on the related topic. The literature review surveys scholarly articles, books, and other sources related to a particular area of research. The review should reckon, describe, summarize, accurately evaluate, and illuminate previous research. It should give a hypothetical base for the research and help the researcher determine the nature of the research. The literature review admits the work of previous researchers, and in so doing, promises the reader that your work has been wellmeasured. It is assumed that by declaring a previous work in the field of study, the author has read, evaluated, and integrated that work into the work at hand.
