Sutra 2. Nikhilam Navatas'caramam Dasatah (िनİखलंनवतʷरमंदशतः)

Meaning: All from nine and the last from ten.

Use: This sutra is used to get a complement number from its functional base.

The second sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Nikhilam Navataścaramam Daśataḥ, translates to “all from nine and the last from ten.” This mental strategy simplifies subtraction by allowing learners to quickly calculate complements relative to the nearest base of 10, 100, 1000, and so on. Rather than following conventional borrowing methods, it promotes a direct and efficient approach to subtraction that is especially useful when dealing with large numbers or values close to base units. This sutra is particularly helpful in reducing the cognitive effort involved in long-step subtraction, making it easier for students to perform these operations mentally.
