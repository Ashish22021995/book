2. Solving Simple Linear Equations

Steps to Apply:

Step 1: Rearrange the equation into the format ax + b = cx + d

Step 2: Apply Parāvartya by transposing terms and reversing their signs.

Step 3: Solve for x.

Example: Solve 8x + 3 = 5x + 6

1. Rearrange: 8x − 5x = 6 − 3 (transpose terms and reverse signs).

2. Simplify: 3x = 3.

3. Solve: x = 3/3 = 1.

3. Polynomial Division

Steps to Apply:

Step 1: Write the polynomial division in synthetic form.

Step 2: Keep the leading coefficient as it is, and reverse the signs of subsequent coefficients based on the divisor.

Step 3: Solve step-by-step until all terms are divided.

Example: Divide x3 − 7x2 + 41 x −35 by x − 1

1. Divisor: x − 1 → Transform by keeping the sign of 1 as it is.

2. Write coefficients of the polynomial:

[1, −7, 41, −35].

3. Perform synthetic division:

The first coefficient (1) remains the same.

Multiply 1 × 1 and add to −7: −7 + 1 = −6.

Multiply −6 × 1 and add to 41: 41 − 6 = 35.
