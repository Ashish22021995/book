Problem: A laboratory sample contains 34g of water (H₂O). The molar mass of water is 18g/mol. Calculate the total number of water molecules in the sample using 34 × 56, where 56 represents Avogadro's constant in simplified form (6.022 × 10 23) divided by 18.

Solution:

1. Given Values:

Mass of water: 34g

Simplified value for (Avogadro’s constant)/(Molar Mass) = 56

2. Apply the Sutra to Calculate 34 × 56:

Write the numbers: 34 and 56.

Step-by-Step Multiplication Using the Sutra:
