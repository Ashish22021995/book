Application of the Sutra in Chemistry: Determining the Number of Ions in a Sample

Problem: A sample contains 42 g of sodium chloride (NaCl). The molar mass of NaCl is 58.5 g/mol. Determine the total number of sodium (Na⁺) and chloride (Cl⁻) ions in the sample using Avogadro's number. Simplify the calculation by using 42×102, where 102 represents 2×6.022×1023 divided by 58.5.

Solution:

1. Given Values:

Mass of NaCl: 42 g

Simplified multiplier for ions: 102 (double the number of moles, since NaCl
dissociates into two ions).

2. Apply the Sutra to Calculate 42×102:

Write the numbers: 42 and 102.

Step 1: Multiply the unit digits:
2×2=4. Write 4 in the unit's place, with no carry-over.

Step 2: Multiply crosswise:
(4×2) + (2×0) = 8. Write 8 in the tens place, with no carry-over.

Step 3: Multiply the tens digits: 4×1=4. Write 4 in the hundreds place.

Combine the results: 4284.

3. Interpretation: The total number of ions in the sample is approximately: 4.284×1023 ions.

Complement (Unreacted mass) = 3258 mg.

Urdhva-tiryagbhyam provides a structured and efficient method for multiplying numbers of any size using vertical and crosswise steps. Its visual approach simplifies large or complex multiplications, making it especially valuable in scientific calculations involving molecular quantities, conversions, and constants supporting speed, accuracy, and confidence in data handling.

Sutra 4. Parävartya Yojayet ( परावर्त्य योजयेत् )

Meaning: Transpose and apply.

Use: This sutra simplifies various types of mathematical operations by "transposing and applying." It is useful in divisions, solving equations, and transforming expressions by rearranging and reversing signs.
