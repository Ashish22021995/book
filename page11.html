Sutra 4. Parävartya Yojayet ( परावर्त्य योजयेत् )

Meaning: Transpose and apply.

Use: This sutra simplifies various types of mathematical operations by "transposing and applying." It is useful in divisions, solving equations, and transforming expressions by rearranging and reversing signs.

The fourth sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Parāvartya Yojayet, meaning “transpose and apply,” serves as a versatile and logical method for simplifying a range of mathematical operations. In mathematics, it allows for simplified approaches to polynomial division and the solving of linear equations, where transposing terms and reversing signs enables quicker, more structured solutions. The strength of this sutra lies in its adaptability across different types of problems.

Within scientific disciplines such as chemistry, Parāvartya Yojayet becomes particularly relevant in quantitative contexts. Whether dividing measured quantities to determine container capacity or applying it in partial pressure calculations and concentration analysis, the sutra supports both accuracy and efficiency. By transforming the way learners approach equations and numerical breakdowns, Parāvartya Yojayet reinforces core mathematical thinking and problem-solving skills that are fundamental in scientific learning environments.
