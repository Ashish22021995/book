Sutra 12. Sesanyankena Caramena ( शेषरायङ्केन चरमेण )

Meaning: The remainders by the last digit.

Use: This sutra is used to express a fraction as a decimal, to all its decimal places. It provides an efficient method to determine the recurring decimal representation of fractions like 1 7 , 1 13 , etc. without performing complete long division. Instead, it uses remainders and multiplications by the last digit.

Key Idea:

1. Division Methodology: The remainders from dividing a number step-by-step are used to generate recurring decimals.

2. Multiplication Methodology: The remainders are multiplied by the denominator (or a "last digit") to determine the recurring sequence directly.

Steps:

1. Long Division for Remainders: Perform the division to record the sequence of remainders.

2. Multiplication Using Last Digit: Multiply each remainder by the denominator (or a key multiplier) to compute the recurring digits.

3. Use Complements: For certain fractions, the recurring sequence can be derived faster using complements from 999 (explained below).

The twelfth sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Śeṣānyankena Carameṇa, provides a distinctive and efficient approach to converting fractions into recurring decimal representations. Rather than depending on long, laborious division, this sutra leverages the patterns formed by remainders and the numerical properties of the denominator to extract recurring digits. By tracking remainders during successive division steps and combining them with strategic multiplications involving the last digit or base of the denominator, this method reveals the full decimal cycle with minimal computation. This sutra is particularly useful when expressing fractions like 1 7 , 1 13 , or other non-terminating decimals especially those with longer recurring cycles.

Its relevance extends to scientific domains as well. In physical chemistry, where mole fractions and concentration ratios frequently yield recurring decimal values, this sutra aids in obtaining exact representations quickly and reliably. Ultimately, Śeṣānyankena Carameṇa reinforces the elegance of mathematical cycles, transforming what appears to be a complex decimal expansion into an intuitive pattern which simply revealed through remainders and repetition.
