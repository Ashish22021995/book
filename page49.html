Sutra 14. Ekanyunena Purvena ( एकन्यूनेन पूर्वेण )

Meaning: By one less than the one before.

Use: This sutra is a corollary of the Nikhilam Sutra and the converse of the Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa Sutra. It simplifies multiplication problems where the multiplier consists entirely of the digit 9 or its equivalent in binary or recurring decimals.

This sutra, a corollary to Nikhilam Navataścaramam Daśatah, offers an elegant approach to multiplication, particularly where the multiplier is composed entirely of 9s (like 9, 99, 999, etc.). It is the inverse of the more commonly known Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa sutra and is especially useful when one of the numbers in the multiplication pair consists of repeated nines. Beyond arithmetic, the sutra is applicable in binary multiplication, where it aligns with numbers of the form 2n - 1, and also finds use in expressing recurring decimals in rational fractions involving the digit 9 or its variations.

In scientific settings, this sutra proves beneficial when scaling fixed units. Whether used for multiplying in decimal or binary, simplifying repeating fractions, or executing scale-up problems in science, Ekādhikena Pūrveṇa reinforces the Vedic principle of simplifying through patterns. It demonstrates how profound insight into number structure can lead to computational clarity and speed, especially where traditional multiplication would require multiple steps.

1. Multiplications with a Multiplier of All 9's

1. When the multiplicand and multiplier have the same number of digits:

Subtract 1 from the multiplicand for the left-hand side of the product.

For the right-hand side, take the complement of the left-hand side with respect to the multiplier (e.g., 9, 99, and 999…).
