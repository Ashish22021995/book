Ānyarūpaye Śūnyamanyat simplifies systems of equations by identifying proportional relationships. If one set of ratios matches, the other variable must be zero. Sutra 7. Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyam ( संकलनव्यकलनाभ्याम् )

Meaning: By addition and subtraction.

Use: This sutra is used to find the value of variables in a system of linear equations if the coefficients of variables are found interchanged. By systematically adding and subtracting the equations, the values of the variables can be determined efficiently.

The seventh sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyam, translates to “by addition and subtraction.” This sutra presents a powerful and systematic technique for solving simultaneous linear equations, especially when the coefficients of variables appear to be interchanged or symmetrical. By simply adding and subtracting equations in a structured manner, this method enables direct elimination of variables.

In scientific contexts, Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyam finds practical application in determining unknown coefficients in stoichiometric relationships, balancing chemical equations, or analyzing molar ratios in reaction mechanisms. The ability to solve systems of equations efficiently is crucial in kinetic modeling, equilibrium analysis, and other areas where chemical species interact in defined quantitative proportions. Ultimately, Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyam not only simplifies numerical computations but also supports logical reasoning, offering learners a reliable tool for algebraic clarity and scientific precision in equation-based problem solving.

Steps for Solving Using Sankalana Vyavakalanābhyam

1. Identify the equations:

Ensure the coefficients of x and y in the equations are interchanged.

2. Add the equations:

Add to eliminate one variable, leaving an equation with the other variable.

3. Subtract the equations:

Subtract to eliminate the other variable, leaving another equation with the first
variable.

4. Solve the resulting equations:

Use the simplified equations from the addition and subtraction steps to find the
values of x and y.
