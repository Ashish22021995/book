3. Recurring Decimals

The sutra can convert fractions where the numerator is 1 into their recurring decimal forms.

Example 1: Multiplicand and Multiplier have the same number of digits

777 × 999

1. Subtract 1 from 777 :

777−1=776(Left-hand side of the product).

2. Find the complement of 776 with respect to 999 :

Complement = 999 − 776 = 223 (Right-hand side of the product).

Answer: 777 × 999 = 776223

Example 2: Multiplicand has fewer digits than the multiplier

79 × 99999

1. Pad 79 with leading zeros to match the multiplier:

79 → 00079

2. Subtract 1 from 00079 :

00079 − 1 = 00078 (Left-hand side of the product).

3. Find the complement of 00078 with respect to 99999 :

Complement = 99999 − 00078 = 99921 (Right-hand side of the product).

Answer: 79 × 99999 = 78999921
