Sutra 15. Gunitasamuchchaya ( गुणितसमुच्ययः )

Meaning: "The product of the sum of coefficients of the factors."

Use: This sutra is a verification method to check the correctness of results in multiplication, division, or factorization. It involves comparing the product of the sum of coefficients of factors to ensure the equality of the expression.

Guṇitasamuccaya is a sutra designed for verification rather than computation. It offers an elegant method to check the correctness of factorizations, multiplications, and divisions without expanding entire expressions. The core idea is simple: the product of the sums of coefficients of each factor must equal the sum of the coefficients of the final expression. This verification tool is especially valuable when working with polynomials and expressions involving multiple terms. Instead of re-expanding or recalculating, a simple summation check provides confirmation of correctness of the solution which is ideal for mental math or high-pressure testing environments.

Beyond algebra, this sutra finds meaningful applications in science. In chemistry, for instance, it can be used to verify rate laws or balance reactions without detailed expansion. By summing coefficients of component terms in a factorized rate equation and comparing it to the coefficients in the expanded form, students can validate their derivation with clarity and precision.
