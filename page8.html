The third sutra of Vedic Mathematics, Urdhva-tiryagbhyam, meaning “vertically and crosswise,” introduces a unique and efficient method of multiplication. This sutra enables quick computation by organizing the multiplication process into vertical and diagonal (crosswise) steps. This sutra offers a more visual and organized approach that minimizes error and streamlines the steps involved. It supports multiplication of numbers of any length by breaking down the problem into manageable parts and following a consistent structure.

In scientific contexts such as chemistry, where large numbers and constants like Avogadro’s number are commonly involved, Urdhva-tiryagbhyam becomes particularly useful. Calculations related to moles, molecular quantities, and conversions often require multiplying large or multidigit numbers. It allows for quick approximations and accurate results in problem-solving scenarios, making it an effective strategy for enhancing computational fluency in science-based applications. Ultimately, Urdhva-tiryagbhyam strengthens the ability of a learner to handle complex multiplications with confidence and ease, both in mathematics and in scientific disciplines where numerical reasoning is essential.

Steps to Apply:

Step 1: Align the digits of the numbers to be multiplied.

Step 2: Multiply digits vertically and crosswise, adding results when necessary.

Step 3: Write the results step by step, ensuring the correct placement of digits for each step.

This method works for numbers of any size but is most commonly illustrated with two-digit numbers.

Example 1: Multiply 23 × 12

1. Write the numbers: 23 and 12.

2. Steps:

  • Multiply the unit digits: 3 × 2 = 6. Write 6 in the unit's place.

  • Multiply crosswise: (2 × 2) + (3 × 1) = 4 + 3 = 7. Write 7 in the tens place.

  • Multiply the tens digits: 2 × 1 = 2. Write 2 in the hundreds place.

3. Result Combine the digits: 276.
